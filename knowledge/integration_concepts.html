What Was Adopted

Three Stitch patterns worth integrating — and the specific reason each earns its place in The Grind.

grid_view
Bento Metric Cards
A 2×2 grid of fixed-height cards, each dedicated to a single KPI: large number, muted label, icon, and a micro bar at the bottom. No borders fighting each other. No scrolling to find your numbers.
Why it works
PrepSDE's original layout buried metrics inside prose-like pace bars. The bento pattern makes data density feel deliberate, not cramped — you scan four numbers in one glance instead of hunting through a list.
show_chart
Burn-Down Trajectory Chart
A full-width inline SVG chart with a dashed ideal-pace line and a solid actual-progress line with gradient fill. A circle marker pins the user's current position in time.
Why it works
A deadline number alone ("87 days") is abstract. The burn-down chart makes it visceral: you can see whether you're above or below the ideal line right now. It converts anxiety into actionable signal.
radio_button_checked
Pace Indicator Pill
A small pill at the very top of the screen with a pulsing dot and a single uppercase verdict: "YOU ARE ON TRACK" or "BEHIND — CATCH UP". Color shifts from green → amber → red based on trajectory.
Why it works
PrepSDE's Dynamic Deadline Engine already computes this verdict internally. Surfacing it as the first thing you see on the home screen closes the feedback loop before the user has to think — one look, one answer.
